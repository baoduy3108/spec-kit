# Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics

Foundational principles for evaluating and designing usable interfaces. Apply these heuristics throughout design — not just as post-launch audits.

## The 10 Heuristics

1. **Visibility of System Status** — Keep users informed through timely feedback. Show loading states, progress indicators, and state changes immediately.
2. **Match Between System and Real World** — Use user language, not internal jargon. Employ familiar conventions so the interface feels intuitive.
3. **User Control and Freedom** — Provide clear exit paths, undo capabilities, and escape routes so users never feel trapped.
4. **Consistency and Standards** — The same action should look and behave identically throughout the product and align with platform conventions.
5. **Error Prevention** — Design to prevent errors first. Use constraints, confirmations, validation, and sensible defaults rather than relying on error recovery.
6. **Recognition Rather Than Recall** — Make options visible in context. Users shouldn't need to remember information across different screens.
7. **Flexibility and Efficiency of Use** — Serve both novices and experts. Provide shortcuts and customization for power users without adding complexity.
8. **Aesthetic and Minimalist Design** — Every element should earn its place. Remove decorative and rarely-needed information.
9. **Error Recognition, Diagnosis, and Recovery** — Error messages should use plain language, identify the problem clearly, and suggest constructive next steps.
10. **Help and Documentation** — Provide contextual, task-oriented help at the point of need rather than burying it in documentation.

Use this as a checklist when conducting a heuristic evaluation of any interface.
