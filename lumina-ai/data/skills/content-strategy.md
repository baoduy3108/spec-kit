# Content Strategy

Content strategy is producing content that **serves a specific audience and advances a business goal** — not just publishing for its own sake. Most content fails because it's created without a strategy: no clear audience, no goal, no distribution plan. Strategy fixes that.

## Start With Audience & Goal

Before creating anything, answer: **who is this for, and what should it achieve?** (Grow an audience? Generate leads? Establish authority? Support customers?) Content only works if it serves a **specific audience's needs/interests** *and* ladders up to a business outcome. Content that serves neither is noise. Know your reader (see product-discovery for understanding them) and your objective.

## Topics & Pillars

Organize around **pillar topics** you want to be known for (aligned with your product/expertise and what your audience searches for), with clusters of supporting pieces. This builds **topical authority** (good for SEO — see seo-content-strategy), keeps you focused, and compounds over time. Depth in a few areas beats scattershot content on everything.

## Match Content to the Funnel

Different content serves different stages of the audience's journey:
- **Awareness (top)** — broad, helpful, educational content that attracts people with a problem (how-tos, guides). Not salesy.
- **Consideration (middle)** — content comparing approaches, case studies, deeper dives for people evaluating solutions.
- **Decision (bottom)** — product-specific content, demos, proof for people ready to choose.
A strategy needs content across the funnel; all-awareness content builds an audience that never converts, all-bottom content has no audience to convert.

## Distribution Over Volume

**Creating content is half the job; getting it seen is the other half** — often the neglected half. A great post nobody reads is worthless. Plan **distribution** (SEO, email/newsletter, social, communities, partnerships) as seriously as creation. **Repurpose** one strong piece into many formats/channels (a post → thread → video → newsletter) to maximize reach per unit of effort. Consistency and distribution beat sporadic volume.

## Measure What Matters

Track metrics tied to the **goal**, not vanity: if the goal is leads, measure conversions/email signups, not just pageviews; if it's authority, track engagement/shares from the right audience. Double down on what works.

## Pitfalls (in understanding/using)

- **Publishing without strategy** — no defined audience or goal → content that serves no one.
- **Ignoring distribution** — creating great content nobody sees; distribution is half the work.
- **Chasing volume/quantity** over quality, focus, and consistency.
- **Vanity metrics** (pageviews) over goal-tied outcomes (leads, retention, authority).
- **All top-of-funnel** (audience that never buys) or all-promotional (no audience) — cover the funnel.
- Scattershot topics → no **topical authority**; focus on pillars.
- Not **repurposing** — leaving reach on the table by using each piece once.
- Being **all about you** — audiences want value/help, not ads; earn attention first.
