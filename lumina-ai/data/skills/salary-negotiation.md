# Salary Negotiation Prep

Negotiation is expected — most employers budget for it, and not negotiating can leave a lot on the table over a career. The goal is win-win, not adversarial.

## What You're Really Negotiating

Total comp, not just base: base salary, signing bonus, annual bonus/commission, equity (RSUs/options, vesting), 401k match, benefits, PTO/remote flexibility, professional-development budget, start date, and title.

**Total comp = Base + Bonus + Equity + Benefits.** Always compare offers on total comp, but remember base drives future raises and loan qualification.

## Research Phase

1. **Market range** from multiple sources (Levels.fyi for tech, Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary, Blind, PayScale). Build a range: 25th / 50th (target) / 75th / 90th (stretch) percentile.
2. **Know your value** — years of relevant experience, rare/specialized skills, track record, in-demand certs, competing offers all push you up.
3. **Calculate total comp** for each offer side-by-side.

## Timing & Framework

Negotiate **after a written offer, before signing.** Never accept on the spot. Counter-offer structure: (1) express genuine enthusiasm, (2) reinforce your value, (3) make a specific ask, (4) justify it, (5) open discussion.

## Counter-Offer Script (core)

> "Thank you for the offer — I'm excited about [specific]. Based on my market research and [X years / key achievement / competing offer], I was hoping we could discuss a base of $[ask] rather than $[offer]. Is there flexibility there?"

If they can't move base: pivot to signing bonus (one-time, easier to approve), equity, an earlier review, extra PTO, or remote flexibility.

## Common Scenarios

- **First offer is low** — express enthusiasm, counter with a research-backed number, be ready to justify.
- **"What are your salary expectations?"** — deflect: "What range have you budgeted?"; if pressed, give a researched range.
- **Won't budge on base** — negotiate the other elements.
- **Competing offers** — only mention if true; frame as problem-solving, never a threat.
- **"What's your current salary?"** — often illegal to ask; redirect to market rate and the value you bring.

## Do / Don't

**Do**: always negotiate respectfully, get the offer in writing first, be specific with numbers, give them a way to say yes, be patient, get the final deal in writing.
**Don't**: accept on the spot, volunteer salary history, make ultimatums, lie about competing offers, or accept verbal promises without writing.
