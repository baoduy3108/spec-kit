# Mentoring & Coaching

Both develop people, but differently — and the most common mistake is doing all telling and no asking.

## Mentoring vs Coaching

- **Mentoring** — you **share your experience, knowledge, and advice**; a more experienced person guiding a less experienced one ("here's what I learned, here's how I'd approach it, here are the pitfalls"). Directive, wisdom-transfer.
- **Coaching** — you **ask questions that help them find their own answers**; you don't need to know the domain better, you help them think. Non-directive, capability-building.
Great developers of people flex between them: mentor when they lack knowledge they need now; coach when they're capable of working it out and will grow more by doing so.

## Ask More Than You Tell

The instinct is to solve their problem and hand over the answer — it's faster and feels helpful. But it makes them dependent and skips their learning. **Asking** ("What have you tried? What's your read on it? What options do you see? What would happen if…?") builds their judgment and ownership. Resist jumping in with the solution; let them reach it, guiding with questions. Tell when the cost of them not knowing is too high (safety, a hard deadline); coach the rest.

## The GROW Model (a coaching structure)

- **Goal** — what do they want to achieve? (make it specific)
- **Reality** — what's the current situation, what have they tried, what's really going on?
- **Options** — what could they do? Generate several before evaluating.
- **Will / Way forward** — what *will* they do, by when? Commit to a concrete next step.
A simple, powerful frame for a coaching conversation that ends in action they own.

## Good Development Habits

- **Meet them where they are** — match your approach to their level and confidence (see delegation's oversight point); don't overwhelm a beginner or over-explain to an expert.
- **Growth-oriented feedback** — specific, behavior-focused, kind-but-direct (see giving-and-receiving-feedback); frame it as investment in them.
- **Stretch, don't rescue** — give challenges slightly beyond current ability and support through the struggle, rather than removing every obstacle.
- **Listen and be present** — mentoring/coaching is mostly listening; make them feel heard.
- **Celebrate progress** and normalize mistakes as learning.

## Pitfalls

- **Telling instead of asking** — solving for them, creating dependence, skipping their growth.
- **One-size-fits-all** — same approach regardless of the person's level/needs.
- **Rescuing** — removing every challenge so they never build resilience.
- **Making it about you** — "when I was your age…" more than about them.
- **Vague feedback** or no follow-through on commitments.
- Coaching when they genuinely need knowledge (or mentoring when they need to work it out themselves) — mismatching the mode.
