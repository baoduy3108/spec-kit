# App Store Optimization (ASO)

ASO is "SEO for app stores" — improving how an app **ranks and converts** in the Apple App Store and Google Play. It has two halves: getting **found** (ranking for searches, appearing in browse) and getting **installed** once found (the listing convinces people to tap "Get"). Most app downloads come from **store search and browse**, so ASO is often the highest-ROI growth lever (see product-analytics ideas, seo-fundamentals for the parallel).

## The Two Goals: Visibility and Conversion

- **Visibility (ranking)** — appearing high for relevant **search terms** and in browse/category rankings. Driven largely by **keyword relevance** and **download velocity + engagement**.
- **Conversion (install rate)** — of the people who see your listing, how many install. Driven by the **icon, screenshots, title, ratings, and reviews**. High conversion also *boosts* ranking (the store favors listings that convert).
Both matter: ranking without conversion wastes impressions; conversion without ranking gets no impressions.

## Keywords (getting found)

- **Title/name** — the strongest keyword signal; include your most important keyword naturally.
- **Subtitle (iOS) / short description (Android)** — secondary keyword real estate and a value pitch.
- **Keyword field (iOS)** — a dedicated 100-char keyword list (not shown to users); don't waste it on the app name or duplicates.
- **Description (Android)** — Google Play **indexes** the long description, so keywords there matter (naturally written, not stuffed). Apple does **not** index the description for search — a key platform difference.
- Target keywords by **relevance + search volume + achievable competition**; long-tail terms are easier to rank for.

## Creative (converting the visit)

The visual listing decides installs:
- **Icon** — the first thing seen; must be distinctive, clear at small size, and on-brand.
- **Screenshots** — the biggest conversion lever. Show the **value/benefit**, not raw UI; use captions; front-load the best (many users only see the first 1–2). Consider a **preview video**.
- **Ratings and reviews** — social proof and a ranking factor. A high star rating dramatically lifts conversion. Prompt happy users to rate (at good moments), respond to reviews, and fix issues that drive 1-stars.

## The Feedback Loop

Stores reward **momentum**: **download velocity** (installs over a short window), **retention**, and **engagement** feed ranking, which drives more impressions, which drives more installs. So ASO isn't static text — improving conversion and retention **compounds** into better ranking. Conversely, bad retention/uninstalls hurt ranking.

## Design Guidance

- **Research keywords** (relevance × volume × competition); use each metadata field deliberately.
- **Platform differences** — Play indexes the description; Apple uses the keyword field. Optimize each store separately.
- **Invest in screenshots/icon** — the highest-leverage conversion assets; A/B test them (Play has native experiments; iOS has product page optimization).
- **Cultivate ratings/reviews** — prompt at delight moments, respond, fix pain points.
- **Localize** the listing for major markets (keywords + creative) — big organic upside.
- **Iterate with data** — track impressions → conversion → installs, and test changes.

## Pitfalls (in understanding/using)

- **Keyword stuffing** → looks spammy, hurts conversion, and can violate guidelines; write naturally.
- Treating both stores **identically** → they index differently (description vs keyword field).
- **Neglecting screenshots/icon** → poor conversion wastes all your ranking effort.
- Ignoring **ratings/reviews** → low stars tank both conversion and ranking.
- **UI screenshots** with no value proposition → users don't grasp the benefit; show outcomes, not chrome.
- Optimizing ranking while ignoring **retention** → the store's engagement signals drag you back down.
- Set-and-forget → ASO is iterative; competitors and algorithms change.
