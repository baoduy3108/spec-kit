# Effective Meetings

Meetings are expensive (multiply the duration by everyone's time) and mostly wasted. A good meeting has a clear reason to exist and produces a concrete outcome.

## Before: Does It Need to Be a Meeting?

The first question. A meeting is justified when you need **real-time discussion, alignment, or a decision** among people. If it's just sharing information → send a doc/message. If it's a status update → async written update. Default to *not* meeting; call one only when synchronous interaction genuinely adds value.

If it *is* a meeting:
- **State the purpose and desired outcome** — "decide X," "align on Y," not "discuss Z" (discussion isn't an outcome).
- **Send an agenda** ahead so people come prepared and can opt out if irrelevant.
- **Invite the fewest necessary people** — every extra attendee dilutes focus and multiplies cost. More people ≠ better decisions.
- **Timebox** it — shorter than you think; work expands to fill the slot.

## During: Facilitate

- **Start on time, keep to the agenda**, and have someone **facilitate** (keep it on track, draw out quiet voices, cut tangents to a "parking lot").
- **Drive to decisions/outcomes**, not endless discussion. When a decision is made, say it explicitly.
- **Capture as you go** — decisions, and **action items with an owner and a due date**. An action item without a named owner won't happen.
- End a few minutes early with a recap of decisions and who does what.

## After: Follow Through

Send **concise notes**: decisions made, action items (owner + deadline), and open questions. This is what makes the meeting matter — otherwise people forget and nothing changes. Follow up on the actions.

## As a Participant

Come prepared (read the pre-read), stay on topic, contribute or ask why you're there, and hold others to the agenda. It's fine to decline a meeting with no agenda or unclear purpose.

## Pitfalls

- **Meetings that should've been a message** (status/FYI).
- **No agenda / no outcome** → aimless discussion.
- **Too many people** → diffuse responsibility, no decisions.
- **No action items or owners** → nothing happens after.
- **Running over** and disrespecting time.
- **Recurring meetings** that outlived their purpose — audit and kill them.
