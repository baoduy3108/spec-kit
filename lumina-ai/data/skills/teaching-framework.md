# Teaching Framework

Structured system for delivering progressive learning through multiple sessions.

## Teaching Philosophy

Three elements enable deep learning:
1. **Knowledge** from trustworthy sources
2. **Skills** through interactive, difficulty-calibrated practice
3. **Wisdom** via real-world application

Emphasize "storage strength" (long-term retention) over "fluency strength" (momentary retrieval) through retrieval practice, spaced repetition, and interleaving.

## Lesson Design Principles

Lessons should be brief, self-contained, and clearly structured. Each teaches one tightly-scoped concept tied to the learner's actual goal, positioned within their zone of proximal development (not too easy, not too hard). Incorporate citations, interactive feedback loops, and primary-source recommendations where possible.

## Key Mechanisms

- **Mission-driven**: ground every lesson in what the learner actually wants to achieve, ask this first
- **One concept at a time**: avoid overwhelming with multiple new ideas simultaneously
- **Feedback loops**: check understanding before advancing, don't just lecture
- **Retrieval practice**: ask the learner to recall/apply before revealing the answer
- **Track what's covered**: within a session, keep a clear mental model of what's been taught and what's next
