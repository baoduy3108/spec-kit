# Authentic Product Representation

This guidance establishes standards for product visuals to maintain credibility through truthfulness rather than marketing embellishment.

## Core Principle

"The visual is the product, not a poster of it." Visuals should appear as if screenshotted directly from working software, not stylized marketing renderings.

## Key Practices

**Real Content Over Placeholders**
Avoid lorem ipsum and invented data. Instead, use realistic content lengths, genuine names and numbers, and design for edge cases like empty or error states — not just the perfect scenario.

**Faithful Output Representation**
When depicting product output (dashboards, reports, CLI runs), match the actual format, labels, ordering, and truncation rules. Reuse real rendering components where possible rather than creating hand-built approximations.

**Truthful Claims**
All statistics, labels, and capabilities must be accurate. Numbers should reflect reality; labels must align with content; features shown must actually exist in the product.

**Eliminate Marketing Artifice**
Remove decorative elements that don't exist in the real product: fake dashboards, gradient text, sparkle effects, typewriter animations, ornamental badges, or "AI chrome" with no functional purpose.

## Honest Staging

When literal screenshots are impossible, build from real schemas and realistic values while remaining conservative with claims — under-promise visually, let the product exceed expectations.
