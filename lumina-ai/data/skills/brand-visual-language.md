# Brand Visual Language

How shape, typography, and iconography communicate brand personality consistently across UI.

## Key Concepts

**Shape Language** conveys tone before words appear. Rounded corners suggest friendly and approachable; sharp corners communicate technical and serious. Border-radius should align with brand personality across all components.

**Scale Matters**: a radius that looks soft on a button may look sharp on a large container. Larger surfaces typically need proportionally larger radius values to maintain visual consistency.

## Reading Existing Brands

Examine the logo, product photography, typography, and competitor positioning to extract actual shape language rather than guessing from visual impressions alone.

## Typography Extension

When brand books lack sufficient detail for screen use (poor legibility, missing weights), add a complementary secondary typeface for body text while preserving the brand font for headings. Two typefaces maximum; three is excessive.

## Iconography Rules

- Use one icon library consistently
- Match stroke weight to brand tone (thin = refined; bold = accessible)
- Apply rounded or sharp corners to icon paths based on brand personality
- Use standard icons for established concepts (settings, search, delete)
- Deploy icons strategically where they reduce scanning time, not as decorative noise

## Consistency Checklist

Ensure border-radius, typeface tone, and icon style align throughout buttons, cards, inputs, modals, and illustrations for cohesive visual identity.
