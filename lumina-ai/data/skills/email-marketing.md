# Email Marketing

Data-backed email marketing across the lifecycle: acquire the right list, segment it, write emails people open, land in the inbox, and measure what matters.

## List Building (consent-first)

Grow the list with explicit opt-in — never bought/scraped lists (they wreck deliverability and violate law). Offer a clear value exchange (lead magnet, useful newsletter). Use double opt-in where deliverability matters. A smaller engaged list beats a large cold one.

## Segmentation

Send relevant mail by segmenting on behaviour (opened/clicked/purchased), lifecycle stage (new / active / lapsing / churned), and stated preferences. Relevance is the biggest lever on engagement — the same email to everyone under-performs a targeted one to the right slice.

## Copywriting

- **Subject line** — the single biggest driver of opens; specific, curiosity or clear value, no clickbait that breaks trust. Preview text complements, doesn't repeat, the subject.
- **Body** — one primary message and one primary CTA per email; write to one person; benefits before features; scannable.
- **From name** — a recognizable human/brand sender beats "noreply".

## Deliverability (land in the inbox)

- **Authenticate**: SPF, DKIM, DMARC configured on the sending domain.
- **Sender reputation**: warm up new domains/IPs gradually; keep complaint rates low; prune unengaged addresses.
- **Avoid spam triggers**: balanced text/image, no misleading subjects, real unsubscribe link, no spammy phrasing/all-caps.
- Monitor bounces and remove hard bounces promptly.

## Compliance

Honor CAN-SPAM / GDPR / local law: clear identity, physical address, a working one-click unsubscribe honored promptly, and a lawful basis (consent) for sending. Compliance is also deliverability — mailbox providers watch complaint and unsubscribe behaviour.

## Lifecycle & Automation

Set up flows that run without manual sends: welcome series, onboarding, abandoned-cart, re-engagement/win-back, post-purchase. Automated lifecycle mail typically out-performs one-off broadcasts because it hits the right moment.

## Analytics

Track **open, click, conversion, revenue-per-email, unsubscribe, and complaint** — optimize for downstream conversion/revenue, not vanity opens (especially as privacy features inflate open counts). A/B test one variable at a time (subject, CTA, send time); keep winners, document the principle.
