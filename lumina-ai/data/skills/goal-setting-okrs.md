# Goal Setting & OKRs

Vague goals ("grow the business", "get fit") don't drive action because you can't tell if you're succeeding. Good goal-setting makes the target **specific, measurable, and few**.

## SMART Goals

A useful checklist for any goal — **Specific** (clear, not vague), **Measurable** (a number tells you if you hit it), **Achievable** (realistic yet stretching), **Relevant** (matters to what you're trying to do), **Time-bound** (a deadline). "Get fit" → "run 5km without stopping by June 1." The measurability and deadline are what make it actionable.

## OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)

A popular framework for ambitious, aligned goals:
- **Objective** — a qualitative, inspiring *what* ("Delight new users so they stick"). Ambitious, memorable, directional.
- **Key Results** — 2–4 **measurable** outcomes that prove you achieved the objective ("increase 30-day retention from 40% to 55%", "cut time-to-first-value from 10 to 3 minutes"). Numbers, not tasks.
Key distinction: KRs are **outcomes/results, not activities**. "Ship feature X" is a task; "raise activation to 60%" is a result. Set the outcome; let the tasks serve it.
OKRs are often set **ambitiously** (hitting ~70% is success) to push beyond the comfortable — but keep some as committed must-hits. They also **align** people: shared, visible OKRs mean everyone rows the same way.

## Leading vs Lagging Indicators

- **Lagging** — the outcome you ultimately want (revenue, weight, retention). Confirms results but too late to steer by.
- **Leading** — the earlier, controllable inputs that *drive* the lagging metric (workouts/week, sales calls, activation steps). Track and act on **leading** indicators — they're your steering wheel; the lagging metric is the rear-view mirror.

## Focus & Review

- **Fewer goals** — a handful you actually pursue beats a long list you ignore. Focus is the point; too many goals = no priorities.
- **Review regularly** — check progress (weekly/quarterly), and **adjust**. Goals are a tool for direction, not a contract to blindly grind; if reality changed or a KR is clearly wrong, update it. Learn from misses.
- **Write them down** and make them visible — unwritten goals drift.

## Pitfalls

- **Vague, unmeasurable goals** — no way to know if you're succeeding.
- **Too many goals** → diffuse effort, nothing achieved.
- **Confusing tasks with outcomes** — "do X" instead of "achieve result Y."
- **Only lagging metrics** — you find out you failed too late to fix it.
- **Set and forget** — no review, no adjustment.
- **Sandbagging** (trivially easy goals) or **impossible** goals that demoralize — aim for stretch-but-believable.
