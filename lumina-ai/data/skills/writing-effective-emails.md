# Writing Effective Emails

The reader is busy and scanning. A good email respects that: it's clear what you want, why, and by when — in seconds.

## Structure

- **Subject line** — specific and informative: "Approval needed: Q3 budget by Fri" beats "Question." It determines whether/when the email gets opened, and lets people find it later.
- **BLUF — Bottom Line Up Front** — put the point/ask in the first sentence, not buried after three paragraphs of context. "I need your sign-off on X by Thursday. Details below." Busy readers get the essential in one line and read on only if needed.
- **Concise, scannable body** — short paragraphs, bullets for lists, **bold** the key ask/deadline. Cut everything that isn't needed. If it's long, it won't be read fully.
- **Explicit action + deadline** — say exactly what you want from each person and by when. "Let me know your thoughts" is vague; "Please approve or comment by EOD Friday" is actionable. Vague asks get ignored.
- **One topic per email** when possible — mixing several requests means some get dropped.

## Recipients & Tone

- **To** = people who must act; **CC** = people who need to be aware but needn't act. Don't make ten people wonder who's responsible — address the owner directly.
- **Reply-all** deliberately — most of the time you shouldn't.
- **Tone** — match the audience and stakes; professional and warm, never passive-aggressive. Written text loses tone, so err toward clear and kind; re-read for how it could be *mis*read.
- **Assume it could be forwarded/screenshotted** — don't write anything in anger or that you wouldn't want seen widely.

## Practical Habits

- **Draft, then cut** — write it, then delete half. The second draft is where clarity lives.
- **Front-load the ask, back-load the detail** (or link/attach it).
- **Proofread** — typos and a wrong name undercut credibility.
- For anything emotional or contentious, **wait and reread before sending** (or pick up the phone — some things aren't email).
- **When not to email** — a quick back-and-forth is a call/chat; a decision needing discussion is a meeting; sensitive feedback is in person.

## Pitfalls

- **Vague subject / buried ask** → ignored or delayed.
- **Wall of text** with no structure → skimmed and misread.
- **No clear action or deadline** → nothing happens.
- **Wrong to/cc** → diffuse responsibility.
- **Sending while angry** → regret.
