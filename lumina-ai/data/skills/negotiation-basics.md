# Negotiation Basics

Negotiation isn't a battle to win — it's finding an agreement both sides prefer to no deal. The skilled negotiator creates value, then claims a fair share, without wrecking the relationship.

## Prepare: Know Your BATNA

Your **BATNA** — Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement — is what you'll do if this deal fails. It's your source of power and your walk-away line: never accept a deal worse than your BATNA, and a strong BATNA lets you push. **Estimate the other side's BATNA too** — it tells you how much room they have. Also set your target (aspiration) and reservation (walk-away) points *before* you start, so emotion in the room doesn't move them.

## Interests, Not Positions

A **position** is what someone says they want ("$50k"); their **interests** are *why* (security, fairness, recognition, a specific need). Positions clash head-on; interests often don't. "I need it by Friday" (position) might really be "I need to demo to the board" (interest) — which opens options a hard Friday deadline hid. **Ask why**, dig for the underlying interests on both sides, and you find trades that a positional tug-of-war misses.

## Create Value Before Dividing It

Don't assume a fixed pie (the **mythical fixed-pie** bias). People value things differently, so **trade across issues**: give on what's cheap to you but valuable to them, and get what's valuable to you but cheap to them (payment terms vs price, timeline vs scope, cash vs equity). Expand the pie with these win-win trades *first*, then split.

## Claiming Your Share

- **Anchor thoughtfully** — the first number frames the range (anchoring is powerful); open ambitiously but justifiably, not absurdly. If they anchor first and it's extreme, re-anchor rather than negotiating from their number.
- **Use objective criteria** — market rates, precedents, formulas — so it's "what's fair by this standard," not a willpower contest.
- **Make the other side a way to say yes** — solve their interests too; a deal they can defend internally is one they'll take.
- **Silence and patience** — don't fill every pause or negotiate against yourself.

## Keep the Relationship

Be **hard on the problem, soft on the person** — firm on your interests, respectful and collaborative toward them. Most negotiations are with people you'll deal with again; a scorched-earth "win" that leaves resentment costs more later. Aim for an outcome both sides feel is fair.

## Pitfalls

- **No BATNA** → you'll accept a bad deal out of fear.
- **Fighting over positions** → deadlock; missing the trades interests would reveal.
- **Assuming a fixed pie** → zero-sum thinking that leaves value unclaimed.
- **Anchoring off their extreme number** without re-anchoring.
- **Winning the deal, losing the relationship.**
- Negotiating while emotional — take a break.
