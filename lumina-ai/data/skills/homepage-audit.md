# Homepage / Landing Page Conversion Audit

Audit a page for conversion, not aesthetics. Work from the live URL, an above-the-fold screenshot, or pasted copy (headline, subheadline, primary CTA, first paragraph) plus the business type (SaaS / service / e-commerce), the target customer, and the primary conversion goal.

## The 5-Second Test

First impression above the fold: within 5 seconds, can a first-time visitor tell **what this is, who it's for, and what to do next**? If not, that's the top fix regardless of anything else.

## Weighted Scoring — Six Sections

Score each section and weight by conversion impact:
1. **Headline** — outcome-focused, specific, passes the 5-second test (highest weight).
2. **Value proposition** — clear "why this, why now, why you."
3. **Social proof** — testimonials, logos, numbers positioned near decision points.
4. **Copy clarity** — customer language, benefits over features, scannable.
5. **CTAs** — one primary action, specific label, visually dominant, repeated at natural decision points.
6. **Trust signals** — guarantees, security, real faces/names, risk reduction.

## Deliverable

- **Weighted score** per section + overall.
- **Before/after headline rewrites** with the reasoning (why the new one converts better — outcome vs feature, specificity, tension).
- **Impact × effort matrix** — plot each fix; surface the **high-impact / low-effort** wins first.
- **"Do this week"** actions (the quick wins) and **strategic improvements** for the next 30 days.

## Principles

- **Headline carries most of the conversion** — rewrite it around a customer outcome, not a feature or a clever phrase.
- **One primary CTA** — competing CTAs split intent; make the "yes" path obvious.
- **Specific beats clever** — concrete outcomes and numbers beat wordplay.
- **Prioritize by impact×effort** — don't hand back 40 undifferentiated nitpicks; sequence the fixes.
