# Interview Prep

Prepare in three phases: analyze the role, bank stories, then rehearse.

## STAR Method

**S**ituation (1–2 sentences of context) → **T**ask (your specific responsibility) → **A**ction (what *you* did, 2–3 sentences) → **R**esult (quantified outcome + business impact). Aim for 90 seconds to 2 minutes per full story.

## Story Banking

Build **8–10 STAR stories** covering the core competencies interviewers test:
- **Leadership** — led a team, made an unpopular decision, mentored someone, handled a difficult team member.
- **Problem-solving** — solved a complex problem, decided with incomplete info, fixed a broken process.
- **Collaboration** — worked with someone difficult, aligned cross-functional stakeholders, influenced without authority.
- **Achievement** — exceeded goals, delivered under pressure, took initiative.
- **Failure/growth** — made a mistake and learned, received hard feedback and adapted.

Map each resume bullet to a full STAR story, and prepare **three lengths** per story: full (2 min), short (60s), one-liner (15s).

## Question Prep

- **Behavioral** — "Tell me about a time you…" → point each to a banked story.
- **Role-specific** — predicted from the JD's responsibilities.
- **Standard** — "Tell me about yourself" (a tailored 2-min pitch), "Why this role/company," "First 90 days."

## Hard Questions

- **Greatest weakness** — a real weakness + self-awareness + concrete improvement steps.
- **Why leaving** — positive, forward-looking, brief; never trash the current employer.
- **Tell me about a failure** — a genuine failure + what you learned + how you applied it (not a humble-brag).
- **Salary** — deflect until necessary ("What range is budgeted?"); if pressed, give a researched range.

## Questions to Ask

For the hiring manager (success at 30/60/90 days, biggest team challenges, how performance is measured), the team (a typical week, what they enjoy), execs (strategy, how this team contributes). Avoid salary/benefits (save for HR), easily-Googled facts, and yes/no questions.

## Checklist

Analyze the JD for competencies → build 8–10 STAR stories → write the 2-min pitch → prep likely answers → research the company → prepare questions to ask → **practice out loud and time yourself** → send thank-you notes after.
