# Creative Director

A creative-direction discipline built on **insight before ideas**. Never jump straight to concepts — mine the tension first.

## Five-Phase Process

**1. Intake** — extract the brief: product, audience, objective, constraints. Match the *scope* of ideas to what's actually being solved (business / brand / campaign / execution) — don't answer a campaign brief with a logo tweak.

**2. Insight (never skip)** — mine the human tension. Use jobs-to-be-done and abstraction laddering to find what the audience *wants* versus what *blocks* them. Output: a one-sentence insight capturing that tension. Weak insight → weak everything downstream.

**3. Ideation** — generate 8–12 concepts using **three different methods from different families**:
- **Structural** — SIT (subtraction, task unification, multiplication, division, attribute dependency), TRIZ, SCAMPER.
- **Association** — Bisociation (force two unrelated frames together), Synectics, Random Entry.
- **Inversion** — Reverse Brainstorming ("how would we make this worse?"), Oblique Strategies.

Method rotation is mandatory — a new method each pass. The first three ideas are warm-up; ideas 5–12 typically outperform them, so push past the obvious.

**4. Evaluate + refine** — a recursive scoring loop:
- **Brief compliance** — pass/fail against the brief's requirements.
- **Idea strength** — weighted: originality 25%, strategic fit 20%, emotional response 20%, feasibility 15%, scalability 10%, simplicity 10%.
- **"Does it matter to humans?"** — a 1–10 human-relevance score.
Stop when the best idea scores high on *both* strength and human relevance, or after ~5 passes. Kill mediocrity — don't advance a 6.

**5. Articulate** — deliver the top 3: each as a one-sentence concept, a visualization, the channels it lives in, its scores, and the rationale.

## Guardrails

- **Simplicity as rigor** — if a concept can't be explained in one sentence, it isn't ready.
- **Empirical saturation** — if an idea's core mechanic is one everyone already uses, cap its originality unless it's structurally novel.
- **Insight is non-negotiable** — an idea without a named tension is decoration.

## Not For

Media planning, final logo design, final copy drafts, or market-research data collection — this is concept direction, not execution production.
