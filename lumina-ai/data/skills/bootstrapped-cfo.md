# Bootstrapped CFO

Capital discipline as a competitive advantage. Profit is a *constraint that forces better decisions*, not just a goal. Every dollar has three costs: direct spend, opportunity cost, and runway impact.

## Unit Economics (survival requirements)

- **LTV ≥ 3× CAC** (best-in-class 7–8×).
- **CAC payback < 12 months** (high performers 5–7).
- **Revenue per employee** as the efficiency scorecard: ~$110–150K at $1–5M ARR, $200–250K at $10–50M, $400K+ at maturity. Bootstrapped firms run 40–70% higher than VC-backed peers.

Violating LTV:CAC or CAC-payback creates a death spiral a bootstrapped company can't survive.

## Cash Management

- **Runway**: 24–36 months minimum; <12 months = danger (control lost). Never fundraise your way out of a cash crisis.
- **Reserves**: operating (3–6 months fixed costs) + contingency (1–2 months) + growth (excess).
- **Burn multiple** = net burn ÷ net new ARR: <1× excellent, >2× concerning; bootstrapped target ~zero/negative (profitable growth).

## Capital Allocation

- Every investment: what's the **payback period**? Target <12 months.
- **Rule of 40**: revenue growth % + EBITDA margin % ≥ 40 (any mix along the line).
- **Hiring**: does it contribute to revenue? time-to-productivity? what else could the salary fund? Never grow a department >50% at once (productivity collapses during training).

## Working Capital

- **Cash Conversion Cycle** = DIO + DSO − DPO; SaaS target negative. Every 10-day reduction frees working capital.
- **AR**: 30–45 day DSO; remind before due, follow up day 1/7/14/30 past due.
- **AP**: pay on the due date (not early) unless the discount beats cost of capital (2%/20-days ≈ 36.5% annualized). Negotiate Net 45–60 after proving reliability.
- **Annual prepay** at 15–20% off: ~30% lower churn, 27–40% higher LTV — customers finance your growth at 0%.

## Forecasting

Use **driver-based planning** (headcount, acquisition rate, churn), not static percentages. MRR buildup: starting + new + expansion − churn = ending. Keep a rolling **13-week cash forecast** updated weekly, and always maintain three scenarios — base, moderate downside (−15–20% revenue), severe (−30–40%) — each with a calculated runway and pre-defined action thresholds (hiring freeze, cost cuts).

## Review Rhythm

Weekly (cash, AR aging, pipeline), monthly (full close in 5–7 days, variance, rolling 12–18 month forecast), quarterly (strategic recalibration, scenario refresh). **Watch customer concentration**: any customer >10% of revenue, or top 5 >25%, is a risk flag.
