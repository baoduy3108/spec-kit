# Cold Outreach Sequence

Spray-and-pray templates don't work. Ten minutes of research + one specific reference = not cold anymore.

## Research First (before drafting anything)

1. Identify the prospect: name, company, role.
2. Search for **recent signals**: funding, a post they wrote, company news, a launch.
3. Assign a **personalization tier (1–3)** from what you found.
4. Note your own positioning (what you offer, proof) and the channel (LinkedIn vs email).

**Signal ranking, strongest → weakest:** recent news event → their LinkedIn activity → company growth data → role + industry awareness only. The stronger the signal you can reference, the warmer the message.

## The Four-Touch Sequence

1. **Connection request** — ≤300 chars, references a specific research signal, no pitch.
2. **First message** — thanks + a bridge from their signal to your relevance + one *soft* question (an invitation to dialogue, not a pitch).
3. **Follow-up #1 (Day 7)** — a *new* angle or value, not "just bumping this."
4. **Follow-up #2 (Day 14)** — possibly shift platform (LinkedIn → email or vice versa).
5. **Break-up (Day 21)** — graceful exit that leaves the door open ("I'll stop here — reach out if this becomes relevant"), optionally naming a resurface date.

## Message Rules

- Every message must reference a **specific research signal** — if it would read identically sent to 100 people, rewrite it.
- Questions are **dialogue invitations**, not disguised pitches.
- Lead with *their* situation, not your product.
- Keep it short; one clear ask per message.

## Self-Critique After Drafting

For each message: does it reference a concrete signal or is it generic? Is the question an actual invitation to reply, or a veiled sales pitch? Would the prospect feel *seen* or *processed*? Rewrite any message that fails.
